Results & Analysis

I modified the Sierpinski triangle code so that I could tint the triangles based on their location in the fractal. I also added code which alters the structure of the fractal. One can imagine the process of creating the next step of a Sierpinski triangle as taking all triangles and punching a triangular hole in them. I modified how this hole gets punched out. In my code, the edges of this hole are not fixed to being half the sidelength, but can instead be any value between 0-1. The relSide parameter of sierpinski_triangle4 controls this. In order to facilitate this functionality, the triangles are no longer equilateral. Instead, their points have to be specified by the points parameter. sierpinski_triangle5 is very similar, but its relSide parameter is random. This randomization is biased towards 0.5, and the intensity of the randomness is governed by the randPar parameter. There was also a cellular automata aspect of this project. My modification here was about changing the rule used after a given amount of steps.
